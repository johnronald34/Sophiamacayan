The Science Behind Climate Change

Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect happens when gases like carbon dioxide and methane trap heat in Earth’s atmosphere, making the planet warmer. This process is natural and necessary for life, but too many greenhouse gases from human activities cause excessive warming.

Arctic Melting

Arctic melting refers to the rapid loss of sea ice in the Arctic region due to rising global temperatures. As the ice melts, it contributes to sea-level rise and reduces Earth’s ability to reflect sunlight, which further accelerates warming.

Global Consequences

  • Biodiversity Loss: Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction.
  • Agriculture: Shifting rain patterns threaten global food security.
  • Sea Levels: Coastal cities face permanent flooding by 2050.
